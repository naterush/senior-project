The Kincade Fire

The Kincade Fire burned 77,758 acres in Sonoma County, California from October 23rd to November 6th, 2019. Unlike the Walker Fire, Kincade threatened over 90,000 structures. It was the largest fire of the 2019 wildfire season.

Vehicles passing through a roadway as the Kincade Fire burns through Sonoma County, Calif., on October 25, 2019.

The fire was first reported from within The Geysers, the world's largest geothermal field. The area was experiencing extreme wind conditions when Kincade started, leading to rapid spread and the eventual destruction or damage of 120 buildings. It also contributed to power outages for residents in large parts of California.

How Can You Help?

The federal government and California's state government support the firefighters, but note that "the danger is not over after the flames are put out". They have responded by investigating damaged structures prone to collapse, road damage, and power line dangers. Large organizations like The Red Cross have provided "relief supplies like overnight shelters and recovery support to those impacted" by the recent wildfires.

While fires occur naturally, the recent string of fires in California have spread more aggressively due to the dry conditions. These dry conditions are partially create by anthropogenic climate disruptions.

Future wildfire likelihood can be reduced by firstly handling flammable objects with care while outdoors. improperly handled cigarettes are a common cause of wildfires. Longer term outlook is improved by following sustainable logging practices and any effort to curb the effects of anthropogenic climate change. To learn more, visit The Center for Climate and Energy Solutions

Charity Navigator ranks The Redwood Empire Food Bank, California Community Foundation, and Habitat for Humanity of Greater Los Angeles as some of the best charities for address California wildfires. Please consider donating.
